4. Mycotoxins — Critical Finding

Parameter Result FSSAI EU US FDA India EU US
Aflatoxin M1 0.164 µg/kg ≤ 0.5 µg/kg ≤ 0.05 µg/kg ≤ 0.5 µg/kg Pass FAIL Pass

Why this matters

Aflatoxin M1 is a metabolite of Aflatoxin B1, a potent liver carcinogen produced by Aspergillus moulds that contaminate cattle feed (maize, groundnut cake, cottonseed). When cows ingest contaminated feed, AFB1 is hydroxylated in the liver and excreted as AFM1 in milk.

Chronic exposure is linked to liver damage and is classified as a Group 1 carcinogen by IARC. The EU sets the world's strictest limit at 0.05 µg/kg — roughly 10× stricter than India and the US (both 0.5 µg/kg).

At 0.164 µg/kg, this sample is 3.3× above the EU ceiling but well within Indian and US limits. The root cause is almost certainly mould-contaminated cattle feed, which can be addressed at the farm level.

LOQ Concern Explained

When a result is reported as "BLQ" (Below Limit of Quantification), the substance was not detected above the lab's measurement threshold. However, if the lab's LOQ is higher than a regulatory limit, we cannot confirm compliance with certainty. The actual concentration could be below the regulatory limit, but the instrument cannot measure that low. This is flagged as an "LOQ concern" and does not indicate a failure — it indicates a measurement gap.
