Watchpoint 1 — Aflatoxin M1

⚠ Most Significant Finding

Aflatoxin M1 detected at 0.273 µg/kg. While legal in India and the US (limit: 0.5 µg/kg), this is 5.4× the EU's strict limit of 0.05 µg/kg.

Parameter Result FSSAI Limit EU Limit US FDA Limit India EU US
Aflatoxin M1 0.273 µg/kg ≤ 0.5 µg/kg ≤ 0.05 µg/kg ≤ 0.5 µg/kg Pass FAIL Pass
Why this matters: Aflatoxins are known carcinogens (IARC Group 1) that come from contaminated cattle feed — specifically mould-contaminated maize, groundnut cake, and cottonseed. When cows eat contaminated feed, Aflatoxin B1 is metabolised in the liver and excreted as Aflatoxin M1 in milk. Aflatoxin M1 cannot be removed by boiling.
Context: The EU uses a 10× stricter standard (0.05 µg/kg) than India and the US (both 0.5 µg/kg) to protect infants and high milk consumers. At 0.273 µg/kg, Heritage is within Indian and US legal limits but significantly above the EU precautionary threshold.

Bottom Line for Parents

🍼 ChoosePure Takeaway

Heritage Toned Milk is safe to consume as per Indian law. 125 of 127 parameters are completely clean — no antibiotics, no pesticides, no heavy metals, no adulteration.

However, if you are giving milk to young children daily, it's worth seeking brands that certify aflatoxin levels below the stricter EU threshold of 0.05 µg/kg. The aflatoxin finding (0.273 µg/kg) is the primary reason this milk scores 82 instead of 90+.
